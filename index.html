AI Is Systems Engineering

The hardest part of production AI agents isn't the model — it's everything around it.

The industry is racing to add large language models (LLMs), and their potential is real: powering systems that make decisions, learn, and adapt continuously. Although models are increasingly capable, what makes them hold up in production is the infrastructure around them.

Platform

A platform-first architecture delivers a compounding effect when operating many AI agents.

Every LLM application has the same engineering layer around the model — verifying outputs, structuring responses, managing context, switching between providers, composing agents. The platform builds that layer once, so every agent on top inherits it.
